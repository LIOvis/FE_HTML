Invader ZIM

A Nickelodeon cartoon from the early 2000s, Invader Zim is about Zim - a small bug-like alien with green skin, pink eyes, and a love for destruction -, Dib - an 11-year-old boy with dreams of becoming a professional paranormal investigator -, and their fight over the fate of all mankind.

ZIM! Irken Invader

Zim is a member of the feared Irken Race - conquerors of large sections of the known universe. Tasked with the subtle infiltration of Earth and given his trusty sidekick, Gir, from the Leaders of the Irken Empire - the Almighty Tallests - Zim set out to complete his mission to prepare the planet for the coming invasion as one of the Invaders responsible for enacting Operation Impending Doom II.

Unbeknownst to Zim however, Almighty Tallest Red and Almighty Tallest Purple have banished Zim under the guise of a mission. During Operation Impending Doom I, Zim accidentally blew up all the other Invaders. Because of this, Zim was banished to the snacking planet: FoodCourtia. Upon hearing the news that the Invaders for Operation Impending Doom II were being given their assignments however, Zim quit being banished and crashed the Great Assigning.

Dib Membrane

Son of the greatest scientist to ever exist, Dib strives to provide the world irrefutable proof of the existence of paranormal entities. Unfortunately for Dib, Professor Membrane - his father and the most forefront expert on all things science - is always wary of his son's so called 'parascience' and often quick to disprove Dib's theories.

After Zim's arrival on Earth, Dib's life quickly begins to revolve around stopping Zim's evil plans. Despite Zim's terrible disguise, most humans are quick to believe Zim when he claims his green skin and lack of ears are the result of a skin condition. Dib is not most people. This newfound paranoia and outspoken claims of one of his seemingly normal peers being an alien quickly begins to isolate Dib from his fellow students even more than before. A combination of consistently being called crazy and a lack of socialization quickly results in Dib becoming more manic than ever before.
